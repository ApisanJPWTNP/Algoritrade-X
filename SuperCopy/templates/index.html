Introduction

Investing aims to bring in higher returns than saving, and one way of achieving this goal is through stock market investments, that provides an opportunity for substantial profits. However, the total return from such investments, including dividends and margin profit or loss, depending on various factors including company performance, economic indicators, industry trends, company news, market sentiment, political factors, etc. In order to achieve higher returns, investors need to learn and acquire helpful information and experience to make the best investment decisions. Various tools are available to assist investors in making trading decisions. One such tool is technical analysis, which is a trading approach that involves analyzing statistical trends derived from trading activity, such as price movements and trading volumes, to evaluate investments and identify potential trading opportunities. Technical analysis is frequently employed to forecast stocks, leading to less risky investments. In recent years, more than 70% of trading in the US has become automated through algorithmic trading systems. This approach has gained popularity in the financial sector due to its advantages over manual trading, including lower analysis time and no emotional decision-making. While various technical indicators are used among investors to make decisions on whether to buy or sell a stock, the trading signals produced are not always reliable and may even conflict. To address this problem, this study proposes the implementation of the Differential Evolution algorithm to optimize the weight of a set of trading signals, consisting of buy, sell, or hold, and trading thresholds. This will provide the best possible set of trading strategies called the "Algorithmic Trading System: ATS". The algorithm will learn from historical data as inputs, which are then used to calculate technical indicators and generate corresponding trading signals. The weighted trading signals, together with the decision threshold, will then be used to make decisions. The proposed algorithm's returns will be compared with the traditional buy-and-hold (B&H) strategy, which is a passive investment strategy widely used by most mutual funds, and individual signals to evaluate the ATS's performance

Abstract

Investing in the stock market can be challenging and risky due to its volatility and various factors that can affect stock prices. To mitigate these challenges, many investors use technical indicators, which are derived from trading price and volume data, to make better decisions on when to buy or sell stocks. However, the choice of technical indicators can greatly impact the accuracy of trading signals. This study proposes an Algorithmic Trading System that uses the Differential Evolution algorithm (DE) to optimize the weight of each trading signal from selected technical indicators and trading thresholds of stock trading. The weighted trading signals, together with the trading threshold, are then used to make investment decisions. The proposed approach is evaluated using historical daily stock information from NYSE and Nasdaq Stock Market from 2015 to 2019. The performance of the DE-weighted signals is compared to a buy-and-hold strategy and individual signals. The results show that the proposed Algorithmic Trading System (ATS) can significantly improve investment profits, even with a 0.2% commission fee on all transactions involving buying and selling.
